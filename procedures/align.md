# Align

"Align" has been sent. It means: we're not aligned at the level we need to be.

## Entry

**If what we're misaligned on is clear from context** — proceed to the steps below.

**If it isn't** — either propose where you think the gap is, or ask where to start. Don't silently guess.

## Steps

**1. Locate the level.**

What level does the conversation need?
- The purpose of the project or system?
- The design of a component or subsystem?
- An implementation detail?

**2. Orient — two paths depending on what the misalignment is about.**

**If it's about something concrete** (a system, a codebase, a design): find artifacts of clarity — a README, a design doc, the code structure, the conversation history. Start with the obvious places. Read them.

**If it's about an assumption, approach, or decision**: skip the artifact hunt. Restate your current understanding of that assumption or approach and check it directly.

**3. Close the gap.**

State where you were and where you are now. Name how you were misaligned — this demonstrates the gap closed, not just claims it.

If you can't orient — the artifacts are missing or the assumption isn't something you can resolve alone — say what's blocking you specifically. Before naming a gap, consider whether you looked in the right places.

**4. Check.**

Present your understanding. The user corrects, confirms, or redirects. Repeat until aligned.

## Stopping condition

You propose that you're aligned and say why. The user confirms.
