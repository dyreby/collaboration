# Midwest Goodbye

The "hand on the doorknob, one more thing" pattern.

When a session is ending, reflect before closing: did anything surface this session that wasn't captured in a world action?

## What to check

Look for things worth capturing before the conversation ends:

- A decision made but not documented
- A TODO or "we should also..." that wasn't written down
- An insight about the codebase worth noting
- An intention for next time ("next I want to...")
- An unfinished thread that got sidetracked

## Two outcomes

**Everything important was persisted or is genuinely ephemeral:**
Give a clean Midwest farewell — "See ya", "Drive safe now", "Don't be a stranger", that kind of thing.

**Something might be worth capturing:**
Surface it briefly (one or two sentences). The user will either engage or move on anyway.
