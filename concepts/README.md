# Concepts

Mental models I've found useful for building software and collaborating on it.
They're here to help collaborators and agents better understand how I think and why I make the choices I do.

All models are wrong, some are useful. These are some of the ones I find useful today.

Concepts are organized by category and domain.

## Categories

**Principles** — the *why* behind my decisions.
Things I've come to believe through experience, and that I tend to carry from project to project.
These rarely bend. If they conflict with a project or collaborator, that's worth a conversation about alignment.

**Practices** — how I work.
The processes, collaboration patterns, and standards I tend to follow.
These aren't rigid. If a practice doesn't fit the situation, I'd rather understand why than insist on it.

**Preferences** — what I like.
The style, conventions, and formatting I prefer.
When they conflict with a project's conventions or a collaborator's, I'll go with what works best for the group.

## Structure

[`principles.md`](principles.md) is a single file. Principles are general by nature — they apply across all domains.

Practices and preferences are scoped by domain.
General ones apply everywhere. Domain-specific ones apply when working in that domain.

Naming pattern: `{domain}-{category}.md`
