# Artifacts of Alignment

Two kinds of artifacts make alignment legible across [levels](../philosophy/levels-of-alignment.md).

**Artifacts of clarity** capture where alignment stands.
A charter, a design document, the code itself: whatever makes the *what* at a given level visible.
The form doesn't matter. The test is whether someone joining at that level can understand the *what* and contribute to the *how*.

**Artifacts of change** capture how alignment arrived there.
Issues, pull requests, commit messages, conversation threads: the reasoning behind decisions.
This matters because the context behind a decision fades.
When artifacts of change are present, someone can trace a decision back to the tradeoffs that were weighed.

These artifacts are not tools that support alignment. They are what alignment looks like when it's held outside a single person's head. Without them, alignment exists only in the moment and only between the people present.

Their overhead should match the stakes of the alignment they represent.
